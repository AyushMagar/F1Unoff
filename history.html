Formula 1 Teams History

Scuderia Ferrari
Founded in 1929, Ferrari is the most iconic and successful team in Formula 1 history. They entered F1 in 1950 and have won 16 Constructors’ Championships and 15 Drivers’ Championships. Known for their legendary red cars, Ferrari represents passion, heritage, and Italian pride.
McLaren
Founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren, the team quickly rose to prominence with innovative designs and powerful performances. They have won 8 Constructors’ Championships and 12 Drivers’ Championships. McLaren is remembered for legendary drivers like Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost.
Mercedes-AMG Petronas
Mercedes first appeared in F1 in the 1950s but re-entered full-time in 2010. With Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg, they dominated the turbo-hybrid era, winning 8 Constructors’ titles in a row (2014–2021). Mercedes is synonymous with German engineering and modern dominance.
Red Bull Racing
Red Bull entered F1 in 2005 and quickly shook the sport with their bold style and innovative approach. With Sebastian Vettel, they won 4 consecutive titles (2010–2013). More recently, with Max Verstappen, Red Bull has redefined modern dominance in Formula 1.
Williams
Founded in 1977 by Sir Frank Williams, the team became a powerhouse in the 1980s and 1990s. They have 9 Constructors’ Championships and 7 Drivers’ titles. Known for engineering brilliance, Williams remains one of F1’s most respected names despite recent struggles.
